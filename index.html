They called me "the squid" at my last job, at a private dental clinic in Gothenburg. Reception, clinical work and administration quickly grew into a bigger coordinating role because of my analogically percieved eight tentacles and natural people skills. Seventeen years in customer service, of which 10 where in patient care, taught me to read people accurately, ask better questions than the obvious ones, and keep my composure when things move fast.

Moving into UX and digital design, feels like the same instincts applied to a different set of problems. While frameworks like the Double Diamond are a solid starting point, real-life processes rarely fit any method completely. My approach is rooted in flexibility, genuine curiosity and a knack for pattern recognition.

That curiosity and pattern recognition is also why I studied frontend development, following my UX degree. I want to understand the whole picture, ship good code in my own products as well as in hand-offs to team mates. Cross-functional work (and potential future leadership roles) requires intersectional understanding and lingustics. Hence the code studies, for this designer.

With colleagues, I try to be direct and dependable. I prefer honest conversations, and I do my best work in teams where that goes both ways. I'm also quite fun to be around, according to reliable sources. When I'm not deep in research or fussing over interfaces, I spend time with my husband, son and our (many) cats. I also binge a lot of movies and tinker with music, which means I'm a splendid quiz mate and office party DJ, when needed.
